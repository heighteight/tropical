Fix $t>0$.
Define $\phi_t:[0,1]\to[0,+\infty]$, $\phi_t(0<x):=-t\ln x$, $\phi_t(0):=+\infty$.
It is bijective with inverse $\phi^{-1}_t:[0,+\infty]\to[0,1]$, $\phi^{-1}_t(0<\alpha<+\infty):=e^{-\alpha/t}$, $\phi^{-1}_t(0):=1$, $\phi^{-1}_t(+\infty):=0$.

Call $\widetilde{\mathbb L}:=([0,1],\tilde+,\cdot)$, where $x\tilde+y:=(x+y)/2$.
This is a commutative non-associative idempotent semiring\footnote{Here by \emph{semiring} I mean a set endowed with an associative multiplication, an associative and commutative addition, with distributivity of the multiplication over addition. By \emph{non-associative semiring} I mean as before but without requiring associative addition. By \emph{idempotent} I mean with $x+x=x$.} with unit $1$ and pseudo-zero\footnote{For a non-associative semiring $R$, a \emph{pseudo-zero} $0$ is an element s.t.\ $x+0=f(x)=0+x$ and $x\cdot0=0=0\cdot x$, where $f$ is a fixed function $R\to R$ not depending on $x$.} $0$.

Call $\mathbb L_t:=([0,+\infty],+^t,\cdot^t)$, where $\alpha+^t\beta:=\phi_t(\phi_t^{-1}(\alpha)\tilde+\phi_t^{-1}(\beta))=-t\ln (e^{-\alpha/t}+e^{-\beta/t})+t\ln 2$,
and $\alpha\cdot^t\beta:=\phi_t(\phi_t^{-1}(\alpha)\phi_t^{-1}(\beta))=\alpha+\beta$.
This is a commutative non-associative idempotent semiring with unit $0$ and pseudo-zero $+\infty$.
$\mathbb L_1$ is usually called the \emph{unit-log-semiring}.

Remark that the bijection $\phi_t:\widetilde{\mathbb L}\to\mathbb L_t$ is an isomorphism of semirings with unit and pseudo-zeros, as $\phi_t(x\tilde+y)=\phi_t(x)+^t\phi_t(y)$ and $\phi_t(x\cdot y)=\phi_t(x)\cdot^t\phi_t(y)$.
Therefore, all the $\mathbb L_t$, for $t>0$, are isomorphic.

Call $\mathbb L:=([0,+\infty],\inf,+)$ the \emph{Lawvere tropical semiring}.
This is a commutative idempotent semiring with unit $0$ and zero $+\infty$.

Since $\mathbb L$ is associative, $\mathbb L_t$ and $\mathbb L$ cannot be isomorphic, for no $t>0$ (this can also be proven, without considering the associativity, by showing that there cannot exist a bijective homomorphism of semiring with units and pseudo-zeros between them).

It can be proven that $\alpha+^t\beta \underset{t\to 0}{\longrightarrow} \min\set{\alpha,\beta}$ (and trivially $\alpha\cdot^t\beta=\alpha+\beta \underset{t\to 0}{\longrightarrow} \alpha+\beta$).
In this sense, one says that $\mathbb L_t \underset{t\to 0}{\longrightarrow} \mathbb L$.
Looking at $\mathbb L$ as this limit is usually referred to as seeing $\mathbb L$ as the \emph{Maslov dequantisation} of $\mathbb{\widetilde L}$ (i.e.\ of the $\mathbb L_t$).

Remark that this limit deforms a \emph{isomorphic family} of commutative \emph{non-associative} idempotent semirings with units and \emph{pseudo-zeros}, to a \emph{non-isomorphic} commutative idempotent semiring with unit and \emph{zero}.
